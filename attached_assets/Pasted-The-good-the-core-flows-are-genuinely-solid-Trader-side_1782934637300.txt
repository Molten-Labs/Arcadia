The good — the core flows are genuinely solid
Trader side: wallet connect → role gate → /terminal (real order book, ticker, positions) → /manage (vault controls, capacity, self-stake) → /reputation (score breakdown with actual Sortino/consistency weighting) → /payouts (HWM-gated profit claims). That's a complete, coherent loop and it uses your real mental model throughout.
Investor side: /traders (marketplace, sortable by score/Sortino/AUM/30d return) → /t/[handle] (public trader profile with score dial, drawdown timeline, deposit flow) → /portfolio (positions, deposit/settle/withdraw activity log). Also coherent, also uses the real model.
One nice detail: /portfolio's withdrawal history shows a "Withdraw request" / "Awaiting window" status — implying a withdrawal cooldown rather than instant withdrawal.
Three real problems I found
1. /dashboard is orphaned and contradicts the rest of the product. Both trader and investor nav configs route their primary "Dashboard" link to the same /dashboard page — but that page is 100% unrelated leftover content: hardcoded mock data (Eval #4821, Active Funded, Profit Target, Daily Drawdown), styled like a prop-firm challenge account (FTMO-style), with zero mention of score, vault, capacity, or HWM, and it doesn't branch by role at all. It reads like a starter template from before the current model was locked in, never removed. This is the single highest-priority fix — it's the first thing both trader and investor personas hit after choosing their role, and it actively contradicts everything else you've built.
2. The capacity formula in the live code doesn't match what we actually decided. /manage shows Score × $1,000 as the capacity formula — that's the original flat linear formula I first proposed, which you explicitly rejected in favor of self-staked capital × multiplier(score) specifically because a pure score-based formula doesn't require self-stake and undercuts the manipulation-resistance story. Right now the live product is running the version you decided against. Worth fixing since it's inconsistent with your reputation-page skin-in-the-game narrative and your whitepaper.
3. The withdrawal cooldown isn't disclosed anywhere in the trust copy. The FAQ on the landing page says investor funds "stay inside a PDA" and imply access is always available (aside from trader restrictions) — but if there's genuinely a withdrawal window/delay, an investor needs to know that upfront, not discover it when they try to pull money out. This is a trust-copy gap, not a code bug, but it's exactly the kind of detail a "finance-literate investor" (the mentor's phrase) will notice and ask about.
Smaller things worth a pass

dashboard, investments, and returns routes are tangled — investments/returns redirect to portfolio, but dashboard is still live and reachable, unrelated to any of them. Worth deciding: kill /dashboard entirely and repoint both nav configs to /terminal (trader) and /portfolio (investor) directly, or repurpose it as a genuine unified overview.
Role is purely a localStorage client-side preference (RoleProvider), not derived from anything on-chain or server-verified — fine for a demo, but worth being conscious that nothing stops one wallet from toggling between trader/investor views. Presumably real permission enforcement lives in the vault program itself, which is the right place for it — just confirm the frontend role isn't being trusted anywhere it shouldn't be.
