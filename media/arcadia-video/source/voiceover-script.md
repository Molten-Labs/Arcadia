# Arcadia Demo Video Voiceover Script

## 90-Second Colosseum Demo

Most managed vaults ask investors for trust. Arcadia requires proof.

Today, a trader can tell a great story, accept capital, and leave investors guessing about real downside. If positions are open and liquid USDC is short, investors can end up waiting for manual action.

Arcadia flips that model. Traders start in paper mode. They risk their own junior capital first. Their public record becomes the qualification layer before investor deposits unlock.

Investors enter through a marketplace built around proof. They see vault status, senior capital, junior buffer, health, strategy, and activity before making a deposit decision.

On the vault page, the product keeps risk at the point of action. Deposit and withdrawal context sits beside NAV, health, buffer, and recent events.

Traders get a different interface. The manager console focuses on operating constraints: graduation progress, junior capital, position limits, and vault status. Investor-only deposit flows stay out of the trader workflow.

Under the hood, Arcadia’s v1 accounting uses USDC base liquidity and WSOL exposure. Pyth-backed NAV values the position. Reserve checks keep liquid USDC available. If a withdrawal needs more USDC, the system is designed to unwind WSOL before payout.

Arcadia is a Solana-native vault layer where capital follows proof, not charisma.
